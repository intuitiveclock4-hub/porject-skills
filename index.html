What we researched

We explored wellbeing through secondary sources, student surveys, and visual design. Each method helped us understand what students need most.

Secondary Research

We reviewed mental health websites and wellbeing studies. Key topics included causes of stress, effects on students, and school-based solutions. We found that students thrive when they feel safe, supported, and connected to their school community.

Primary Research

We surveyed students and teachers across years 5–10. Students shared how they cope with stress, what support they need, and what schools are doing well. The most common stressors were exams and social pressure. Students valued talking to someone, taking breaks, and having flexible deadlines.

Design Ideas

We created a wellbeing app prototype called “B” with posters to promote it. The app includes a wellbeing tracker, anonymous feedback, and daily check-ins. Posters introduce the app, explain its features, and share testimonials from beta testers. We also designed a flowchart showing how students, teachers, and schools interact with the app to improve wellbeing.

Key findings

Our research revealed important insights into student wellbeing. These findings shaped our design and recommendations.

Stress levels

Over 70% of students reported feeling stressed weekly, especially during exams and deadlines.

Support availability

Only 40% of students felt confident accessing support. Most were unsure who to talk to or feared judgment.

Helpful strategies

Students found talking to someone, taking breaks, and using wellbeing apps helpful. Teachers recommended structured routines and peer support.

Student suggestions

Students suggested wellbeing hubs, flexible homework policies, anonymous feedback tools, and more inclusive clubs and events.

Resources & ideas
